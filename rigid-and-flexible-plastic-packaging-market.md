<p><p>Rigid and flexible plastic packaging are two distinct categories within the packaging industry. Rigid plastic packaging includes containers that maintain their shape, such as bottles, jars, and boxes made from materials like PET, HDPE, and polypropylene. These products are characterized by their durability and structural integrity, making them ideal for a wide range of applications in food, beverages, personal care, and household items. </p><p>In contrast, flexible plastic packaging encompasses materials that can bend and conform to the contents, including pouches, bags, and films. This type of packaging is lightweight, often uses less material, and can be designed for efficient storage and transportation. It is widely used in food products, snacks, and medical supplies, benefiting from advancements in barrier properties and sealing technologies.</p><p>The Rigid and Flexible Plastic Packaging Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 9.3% during the forecast period. Key trends include the incorporation of biodegradable materials, smart packaging solutions, and enhanced recyclability aimed at reducing environmental impacts and improving supply chain efficiencies.</p></p>
