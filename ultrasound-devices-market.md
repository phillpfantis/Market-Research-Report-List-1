<p><p>Ultrasound devices are classified into several types based on imaging capabilities. 2D ultrasound provides flat, two-dimensional images, commonly used for general diagnostics. 3D ultrasound creates three-dimensional images, enhancing visualization of structures, especially during pregnancy. 4D ultrasound adds real-time motion to 3D images, allowing for dynamic visualization of fetal movements. Doppler ultrasound measures blood flow and can assess vascular conditions through color-coded displays. Each type serves specific clinical needs, enhancing diagnostic accuracy and patient outcomes.</p></p>
