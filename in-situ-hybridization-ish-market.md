<p><p>In-situ Hybridization (ISH) is a technique used to detect and localize specific nucleic acid sequences within fixed tissues or cells. The market is divided into two main types: Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization (FISH), which utilizes fluorescent probes to visualize target sequences, allowing for high sensitivity and multiplexing capabilities; and Chromogenic In Situ Hybridization (CISH), which employs chromogenic substrates to produce a colored signal, offering a more straightforward visualization method. Both technologies are essential for research and clinical diagnostics.</p></p>
