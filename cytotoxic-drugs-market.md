<p><p>Cytotoxic drugs are integral in cancer treatment, classified into several types. Alkylating agents work by damaging DNA, preventing cancer cell division. Antitumor antibiotics inhibit the growth of cancer cells by interfering with DNA and RNA synthesis. Antimetabolites mimic natural substances, disrupting cell metabolism and replication. Plant alkaloids, derived from plants, interrupt cell division by targeting the mitotic spindle. Other agents include diverse compounds that do not fit traditional categories, each contributing uniquely to cytotoxic therapies.</p></p>
